Donating blood

30th August 2016 Anna

The recent Italian earthquake has risen awareness on the importance of blood donation. For those of you interested in this, let me give you some basic info of how it works in Italy:

_ Blood donation implies no risk for the donor. Every person in good health, weighting 50kg or more, and between 18 and 65 years of age is a potential blood donor. Men can donate up to 4 times per year, while women can donate twice a year.

_ If you are a first-time donor in Florence, you will have to undergo a medical visit which will also include a blood test (everything is of course free of charge). You will be able to donate blood once you receive the results of the medical visit (it will take a few days for the results to be ready)

_ It is highly recommended to book an appointment both for the first medical visit and for each donation afterwards

_ See also further info about blood donation on the World Health Organization webpage

_ In Florence you can donate blood here:

  •  Careggi Hospital
  •  Meyer Pediatric Hospital 
  •  AVIS IOT
  •  Fratres Varlungo.  EUI Visiting Fellow Alessia can help you out with the booking at Fratres Varlungo in English.

If you have some questions, please do not hesitate to let me (or Alessia). We are happy to reply by email or we can also meet and talk over a coffee.

Give blood, share life!
